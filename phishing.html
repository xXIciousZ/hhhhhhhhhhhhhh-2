WHAT IS PHISHING?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals pretend to be trusted organizations or individuals to steal sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, and personal data.


How phishing works

• Fake emails, messages, or websites trick users.
• Victims are lured into clicking links or entering info.
• Hackers gain access to accounts or data.


Important facts

• 90% of data breaches start with phishing.
• Attackers now use AI to create convincing messages.
• Includes smishing (SMS), vishing (calls), and fake login pages.
• 3.4 billion phishing emails are sent daily.


Effects of phishing

• Identity theft
• Financial loss
• Compromised accounts
• Malware infections
• Business data leaks and reputation loss
